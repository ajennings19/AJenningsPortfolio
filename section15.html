Owlet — Brand Evolution & Dream Sight Launch


Scope: Brand design · Packaging design · Art direction · Campaign design (social, email, paid) · Web creative direction · Photography & video direction · Agency Collaboration and Coordination


Owlet was ready to grow up. With the launch of the Dream Sight 3 camera, the team had an opportunity to do more than release a new product — it was a moment to mature the brand, unify the system, and show up with intention across every touchpoint, from the shelf to the screen.

As lead senior brand marketing designer and art director, I drove the creative across packaging, digital, and campaign — working across internal teams and coordinating with an external agency on video and photography production.

I shifted the brand toward something warmer: a brand that lives in the home, in everyday routines, visually woven into life with a new baby rather than standing apart from it.

New Global Dream Product Packaging

  ◾  Design   ◾  Art Direction

  ◾  Design   ◾  Art Direction
  ◾  Design   ◾  Art Direction

The challenge: design a packaging system that could flex across global markets — accommodating language requirements region by region — without losing visual coherence or brand quality.

The Dream Sight 3 launch required a new global packaging architecture — one system that could work across markets, adapt to software updates without a full reprint, and hold its own in a baby tech category growing increasingly homogenous. The redesigned packaging uses Owlet's logo as a bold billboard, hardware and software features called out with clarity and simplicity, and a front-of-box layout engineered around real retail constraints like spider wraps. A regional QR code on the back routes shoppers to deeper product education by market.

The result: reduced packaging costs globally, stronger shelf visibility at retail, and a consistent brand presence across digital and physical for the first time.
